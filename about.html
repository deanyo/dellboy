why does this exist?

there are dozens of "del boy drinks" lists online, but most of them are wrong. they mix up episodes, include drinks that were never actually ordered, and repeat fan myths as fact.

this project started as a simple question: what did del actually drink? to answer it properly, we read every script from all 64 episodes of only fools and horses (1981-2003), searched for every drink mention, and cross-referenced against the official episode list.

the result is 44 confirmed drinks — the most complete and accurate list anywhere online.

how we verified entries

every confirmed drink was found using this priority system:

1 broadcast episode dialogue (what was actually said on screen)
2 dvd subtitles
3 original shooting scripts (66 files covering all episodes)
4 fan transcripts (lowest priority, used only for cross-reference)

we searched every script for drink-related keywords, read the surrounding context to confirm it was an actual order (not just a passing mention), and matched each drink to its correct episode using the official episode masterlist.

why scripts sometimes differ from broadcast

only fools and horses used a live studio audience for interior scenes (the flat, the nag's head, sid's café) while location scenes were pre-filmed without an audience. this means:

ad-libs david jason occasionally improvised drink orders for bigger laughs from the studio audience. the script for heroes and villains says "manhattan" but on screen del orders a "baileys and cherryade".
rehearsal changes jokes were tweaked after rehearsal if a different line got a better reaction from the audience.
prop availability sometimes drinks changed because of what bottles the prop department had on hand.
script versions most scripts available online are early shooting drafts or fan transcriptions, not the final broadcast dialogue.

where we found a difference, the broadcast version takes priority. we note the script variant in our data.

what counts as confirmed vs candidate
confirmed found in at least one script or verified from broadcast footage, with a specific episode reference. currently 43 drinks.
candidate reported by fans or appears in online lists, but not found in any of our 66 script files or broadcast verification. currently 2 drinks (harvey wallbanger and creme de menthe and tizer).

several drinks that were widely believed to be fan myths turned out to be real once we checked the scripts — including baileys and cherryade (ad-libbed), irish coffee (strangers on the shore), and brandy and andrews (sickness and wealth).
